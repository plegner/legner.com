Biography

Henrike is a 21-year old German soprano, who is starting her Master’s degree at the Conservatorio di Musica Cesare Pollini in Padova in November 2019, studying with Cosetta Tosetti. She completed the first three years of her undergraduate degree at the Trinity Laban Conservatoire of Music and Dance in London. After an Erasmus exchange, she stayed in Padova to complete her Bachelor degree in July 2019.

Since early childhood, Henrike has played the violin and the piano. At the age of 8, she joined the children’s choir at the “Deutsche Oper am Rhein” in Düsseldorf. During the following years, she took part in operas including “Hänsel and Gretel” (Humperdinck), “Louise” (Charpentier), “The Cunning Little Vixen” (Janacek) and “Boris Godunov” (Mussorgsky). She also was the lead in Peter Maxwell Davies’ opera “Cinderella”.

Henrike started singing lessons with Dorothee Wohlgemuth when she was 12. During the next few years, she won multiple 1st and 2nd prizes in the national final of “Jugend Musiziert”. Other performances included Bastienne in “Bastien und Bastienne” (Mozart).

In 2013, Henrike received a Music Scholarship from Shrewsbury School, where she was the Solo Soprano in “Messiah” (Handel), “Nulla in mundo pax sincera” (Vivaldi), “Vesperae Solennes” (Mozart) and Susanna in “Le Nozze di Figaro” (Mozart). At the age of 17, she also performed “Jauchzet Gott in allen Landen” (Bach) in Wigmore Hall.

In the first years of her undergraduate degree, Henrike performed several lunchtime recitals at the Old Royal Naval Chapel in Greenwich. With the Burgate Singers and Sinfonia, she was the soloist in “Exsultate Jubilate” (Mozart), “Gloria” (Poulenc), “Nelson Mass” (Haydn) and “Stabat Mater” (Szymanowski). With the Shrewsbury School Community Choir, she was the soloist in “St John Passion” (Bach).

In 2017, Henrike sang Drusilla in “L’Incoronazione di Poppea” (Monteverdi) with the Trinity Laban Opera, conducted by Nicholas Kraemer and directed by Harry Fehr. In 2016 she won the Collingwood Finalist Prize at the Kathleen Ferrier Society Bursary for young singers.

Other engagements as solo soprano include works by Praetorius with the Bach Ensemble Niederrhein, Bach’s “Cantata BWV 35” as well as the Chorus (one to a part) in “Orfeo e Euridice” (Gluck) with the New Generations Festival Company in Firenze, Milan and Piemonte.

During the last year, Henrike took part in many masterclasses including the Eppaner Liedsommer with KS Angelika Kirchschlager and KS Brigitte Fassbaender, Scuola di Belcanto with Joan Patenaude-Yarnell (Curtis Institute of Music), Matthew Rose (ROH, Met), David Syrus (ROH) and Louisa Muller (Met), the Sommerakademie Kloster Ettal with Prof. Christiane Iven and Dartington International Summer School with Emma Kirkby.

In August 2019, she will take part in the International Summer Academy Salzburg with Prof. Christiane Iven, as well as the Masterclass with Edith Mathis at the Schubertiade, Schwarzenberg. In September 2019 she will once again take part in the Eppaner Liedsommer with KS Brigitte Fassbaender.

In 2018 Henrike started a Lied-Duo with the pianist Sofia Andreoli in Padova. They have already performed several recitals and will take part in the Udo Reinemann International Masterclass for Duos under the direction of Christianne Stotijn at the La Monnaie in Brussels, from September 2019 until April 2020.

During her time at Trinity Laban, Henrike was a Choral Scholar at the Old Royal Naval College Chapel Choir with Ralph Allwood and a member of the Trinity Laban Chamber Choir with Stephen Jackson, where she performed many solos.

Deutsche Version

Henrike Legner ist eine 21-jährige Sopranistin aus Deutschland. Bereits seit frühester Kindheit spielt sie Violine und Klavier und war Mitglied des Kinderchores der Deutschen Oper am Rhein in Düsseldorf.

Mit zwölf Jahren nahm sie Gesangsunterricht bei Dorothee Wohlgemuth und gewann mehrere erste und zweite Preise beim Bundeswettbewerb „Jugend Musiziert". 2013 erhielt sie ein Stipendium für das Internat Shrewsbury School in England, wo sie im Alter von siebzehn Jahren "Jauchzet Gott in Allen Landen" (J. S. Bach) in Wigmore Hall, London sang.

Henrike begann ihr Bachelorstudium am Trinity Laban Conservatoire in London, wo sie unter anderem die Rolle der Drusilla in „L’Incoronazione di Poppea" (Monteverdi) mit der Trinity Laban Opera, unter der Leitung von Nicholas Kraemer und Harry Fehr, sang.

2016 gewann sie den Collingwood Finalist Prize beim Kathleen Ferrier Society Bursary für junge Sänger.

Als Solistin sang Henrike unter anderem in Konzerten in der Old Royal Naval Chapel (London), mit den Burgate Singers and Sinfonia (London), mit dem Shrewsbury School Community Choir (Shrewsbury) und beim New Generations Festival (Florenz).

Außerdem nahm sie an verschiedenen Meisterklassen teil: Eppaner Liedsommer mit KS Angelika Kirchschlager und KS Brigitte Fassbaender, Dartington International Summer School mit Emma Kirkby, Scuola di Belcanto mit Joan Patenaude-Yarnell (Curtis Institute of Music), Sommerakademie Kloster Ettal mit Prof. Christiane Iven.

Im Sommer 2019 nimmt sie im Rahmen der Schubertiade am Meisterkurs mit Edith Mathis teil, sowie an der Internationalen Sommerakademie Universität Mozarteum, Salzburg mit Prof. Christiane Iven und dem Eppaner Liedsommer mit KS Brigitte Fassbaender.

Nach einem Erasmus Austausch wechselte sie 2018 an das Conservatorio Cesare Pollini in Padova wo sie bei Cosetta Tosetti studiert. Im Juli 2019 hat sie dort ihr Bachelor Studium beendet und wird im November ihr Master-Studium beginnen. Des Weiteren gründete sie ein Lied-Duo mit der Pianistin Sofia Andreoli und wird ab September an der Udo Reinemann International Masterclass für Duos in Brüssel unter der Leitung von Christianne Stotijn teilnehmen.
